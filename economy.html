ECONOMY

Known as the trading hub of the Province of Ilocos Sur, Philippines, the City of Vigan is supported by its diverse economy through local trade, heritage tourism and agriculture. The fertile lands around the city provide abundant agricultural products such as rice, corn, tobacco, livestock, and poultry, making agriculture its main income-generating industry.

Its well-preserved Spanish Colonial architecture and recognition as a UNESCO World Heritage City draws a consistent stream of tourists. This also generates a large amount of revenue for the city’s hospitality, regional crafts, and heritage-related enterprises and activities like the city tours. Additionally, local businesses and trades and commerce help boost the city’s economic stability, assuring its continued success.
