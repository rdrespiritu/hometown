ATTRACTIONS

Welcome to Vigan, a mesmerizing city where history echoes through the cobblestone streets and time seems to have stopped. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Vigan lies tucked away in the center of the Philippines and is a testimony to the rich cultural legacy of the nation. Experience the captivating fusion of Spanish and Asian elements that characterize this singular location as you meander around its exquisitely maintained colonial structures. Every area of this city reveals a different story from centuries ago, from the striking Calle Crisologo to the magnificent Vigan Cathedral. Come explore Vigan's ageless charm with us and learn why it's still a must-see location for anybody looking to get a peek of the Philippines' colorful past.

The City of Vigan, renowned for its rich cultural legacy and historic beauty, provides visitors with a variety of well-known tourist sites and activities to enjoy:

Calle Crisologo

Take a stroll around this well-known street in the heart of the city. It is a well-known cobblestone street dotted with intact Spanish colonial structures.

Vigan Heritage Village

Discover this UNESCO World Heritage site which includes many historic buildings and museums highlighting the right history of the city.

St. Paul’s Metropolitan Cathedral

Visit this magnificent Baroque-style Cathedral a major historical and religious structure in Vigan.

Syquia Mansion Museum

Experience the late-president Elpidio Quirino's ancestral home which features historical antiques and mementos.

Baluarte

Enjoy the view in this recreational area with a mini zoo featuring a range of animals including tigers, reptiles, and birds.

Crisologo Museum

a museum devoted to the history and achievements of a well-known political clan, the Crisologo Family.

Hidden Garden

a restaurant in a nicely designed garden providing guests with a relaxing ambiance.

Pagburnayan

Visit this well-known jar factory and experience how traditional pottery is made by skilled craftsmen who produce Vigan’s distinctive burnay clay pots.

Bantay Bell Tower

Get a comprehensive perspective of Vigan City and its surroundings by visiting this iconic structure.

Horse-drawn Carriage

A must-try activity giving visitors a tour along the city giving a unique perspective of Vigan City in a horse-drawn carriage, locally known as “Kalesa.”

Hotel Luna

Set at the heart of the Heritage Village in Vigan City, listed as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO, Hotel Luna is the first museum hotel in the Philippines.

Padre Burgos’s House

Explore the historical charm of Padre Burgos House in Vigan, where the architecture has been conserved and the fascinating stories of one of the national heroes of the Philippines reverberate with the sounds of the past.
